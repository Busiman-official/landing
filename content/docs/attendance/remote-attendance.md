Not every workday happens at a desk. When the app can't verify you're on the office network, it asks one short question instead of just refusing the check-in.

## Marking a remote attendance

![Remote attendance sheet with a marking-from selector and a note field](/images/docs/attendance/remote-justification.png)

You're asked to pick where you're marking attendance **from**:

- **Home**
- **Client Site**
- **Travel**
- **Other** — with a free-text field for anything that doesn't fit the above

Alongside that, you write a short note, and the app captures your current location automatically. All of it — reason, note, and location — goes with the check-in or check-out request for a manager to review.

> **Tip:** A specific note ("At Rajesh Traders for the AMC visit") gets approved faster than a vague one ("outside"). Approvers see the note before anything else.

## How approval works

Remote attendance doesn't block your check-in — it's recorded immediately as **pending**. What's pending is whether it counts as *official* attendance, not whether the app lets you clock in.

Check-in and check-out are approved **separately**. It's entirely normal for a check-in to be approved and a check-out from the same day to still be pending — Busiman doesn't assume the two go together.

Each pending request carries:

- Who submitted it, and which department/branch
- The date and which leg (check-in or check-out)
- The note, marking-from reason, and submission time

A request ends up **approved**, **rejected** (with a reason visible to the employee), or **cancelled** if the employee withdraws it.

## Where approvals happen

- **Mobile** — from the **Alerts** tab. Attendance-approval notifications carry approve/reject actions directly on the card — no need to open a separate screen.
- **Desktop** — from the attendance dashboard, visible to Managers, HR, and Admins, which lists every pending request across the branches they oversee.

![Mobile alert card with a pending attendance approval and approve/reject actions](/images/docs/attendance/approval-alert.png)

> **Note:** Only a branch's assigned manager, or HR/Admin, can approve attendance for that branch. Regular employees never see other people's pending requests.
