Every office we've worked with starts the same way: an attendance register at the front desk, a sales diary in the drawer, and a stock notebook that is always one page behind reality.

It works, until it doesn't. A supplier calls asking about a pending payment and nobody can find the entry. An engineer finishes a service call in the field and the report reaches the office three days late, written on the back of an old bill.

Busiman started as an answer to one question: what if every one of those registers was the same app, on the same phone the whole team already carries?

## What moved first

Attendance and call logging moved first, because they are the two things a business owner checks every single day. Once those were live, inventory and sales followed naturally, since a sale should already know what it took out of stock.

## What stayed the same

The workflow. Nobody had to learn a new way of taking an order or marking someone present. Busiman just removed the part where it had to be written down twice.
